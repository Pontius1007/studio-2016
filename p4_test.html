Our client is a former musician turned producer, Peter Hansen, that now runs his own music studio. As a former artist, Peter has high demands when it comes to creativity and is expecting a website with a nice design and artistic layout. After some brainstorming via skype, we feel that we have achieved a good connection with Peter and a mutual understanding of what we all want the website to be.

Testing document

  1. In the first section of the questionnaire the user is asked questions about his choice of webbrowser, operating system and connection speed. Lastly the user is asked about his age in order to get data on the demographics of the users.
  2. In the second section, the user is asked about his impression of the website, which age group he thinks it's aimed at and what purpose he thinks the website is trying to fulfill.
  3. In section three, the user is asked to rate the design and the how easy the site is to navigate. After this the user is asked if he ever had trouble finding something, if he felt anything was placed in an illogical manner and if there is anything with the website's design he would like to have changed.
  4. In the final section, the user is asked to rate to what extent he feels the website has fulfilled his expectations and if there is anything at all about the website he would have liked to have changed. Finally the user is asked to rate the questionnaire itself.

Test audience

In order to get some feedback on our design, a Google form was created asking questions explained in the test document section. This form was spread to mainly coworkers and room mates, but also to the Northern Wave recording studio owners and their friends. Doing it this way, we felt that the base of testers would not only consist of other students and tech savvy persons, but also musicians and business men.

Test platforms

Most of our test users (55.6%) were using windows operating systems, 22.2% were on OS-x and 11.1% were using linux or other. Most of the test user were using google chrome, the second two most popular browsers were safari and internet explorer. Firefox and Opera was the least used browsers. See below for the full statistic.

Feedback and summary

First impressions

The majority of the users liked the website and had a good first impression of it.

The website's purpose

Almost everyone assumed the website's purpose was to inform the public about the Northern Wave music studio, which was our intent.

The design and navigability

On a scale of 1-5 rating the design and the site's navigability , more than 90% of testers rated it 4 or more. Almost 80% of test users had no trouble finding the elements they were looking for.

Suggested changes

When asked if the test user would want to change anything about the design, we got a mixed response. Some wanted to change the colors, others wouldn't and one left a puzzling response of "yes".

Expectations and general changes

Most test user felt the website fulfilled their expectations, and few had any comments on what to change.

Summary

Overall, the website was well recieved and test users had little feedback on things they would want to be different. We are very pleased with the fact that the inherrent intent with the website was well communicated and recieved.
